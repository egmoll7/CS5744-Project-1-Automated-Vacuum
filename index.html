Background

Cleaning can be a daunting task, especially when your household has multiple levels, which at some point in most Americans lives is going to almost be a given. If people are looking for a larger space, they are going to look for space vertically, meaning more levels in the house. Living in a city where you are sharing a house, or in a townhouse makes an autonomous vacuum cleaner that can only do one level is not an attractive purchase. Buying a product that is a few hundred dollars with the ability to clean only thirty percent of the house that you live not a good option. The market has seen the evolution of autonomous vacuum cleaners as a revolutionary idea, with an unfortunate draw back for the majority of people with homes that are more than one level (See Figure 1).

While the autonomous vacuum has been evolving to be an effective device cleaning any space the first device is targeted at only a specific end users. The use of sensors to read its environment has been changing over the years, types of mostly laser or infrared sensors. These sensors allow the autonomous vacuum to trigger programmed behaviours that determine how the device will behave (See Figure 2). Using a methodical back-and-forth motion, the implementation of a cliff sensor will allow the autonomous vacuum to detect the area of the steps and proceed with the action of climbing the steps. With the next few sections we will show how the autonomous vacuum cleaner can be a house staple, with just fixing its inability to traverse steps.
